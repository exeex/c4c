# 203 Phase D2 retained-surface consumer switch analysis

## Goal

Analyze which single retained public fallback/oracle surface, diagnostic/oracle
replacement prerequisite, or retained target/prepared policy boundary should
become the next bounded implementation idea after Phase C2 found no
micro-contraction-ready cache/API surface.

This is Phase D2 of the BIR/prealloc thinning program. It is analysis-only. It
must produce follow-up ideas for later execution and must not directly migrate
consumers, delete prepared APIs, privatize caches, or open prepared-module
retirement work.

## Why This Exists

Phase C2 closed in
`ideas/closed/202_phase_c2_selected_adapter_cache_contraction_readiness_audit.md`.
Its durable artifact,
`docs/bir_prealloc_fusion/phase_c2_selected_adapter_cache_contraction_readiness.md`,
found no accepted bounded micro-contraction candidate from the current Route 3
through Route 7 selected adapter and diagnostic evidence.

C2 explicitly retained the touched surfaces as one of:

- public fallback/oracle;
- target/prepared policy;
- diagnostic/oracle replacement prerequisite;
- transient pass context;
- blocked aggregate work.

D2 must therefore continue from retained-surface facts, not from broad
cache/API migration, aggregate `PreparedFunctionLookups` retirement, broad
`PreparedBirModule` retirement, draft 155, or selected-adapter greenness.

## Direction

D2 decides the next safe one-surface follow-up ideas. It does not implement
them.

For each C2-retained surface that appears plausible for next work, D2 must
state whether the next work would be:

- semantic reader migration;
- diagnostic/oracle replacement;
- public fallback removal after replacement evidence;
- retained-owner adapter clarification;
- target/prepared policy ownership migration;
- no-action because the surface is aggregate, policy-owned, or still blocked.

Each accepted follow-up idea must name exactly one prepared surface or one
diagnostic/oracle row, the retained fallback or policy boundary, the route fact
or retained-owner evidence to use, and the proof shape needed before any later
contraction claim.

## In Scope

- Route 3 memory/source retained fallback and oracle surfaces.
- Route 4 publication identity versus publication mechanics/output surfaces.
- Route 5 edge/join-source identity versus move/edge-publication policy
  surfaces.
- Route 6 call-use source identity versus ABI/call-plan policy surfaces.
- Route 7 comparison provenance diagnostics versus branch-policy,
  printer/debug, and string-authority surfaces.
- `PreparedFunctionLookups` pass-context and aggregate coupling only as
  blockers or as a source of one-surface follow-up boundaries.
- `PreparedBirModule` only as a blocker and non-goal, unless D2 produces a
  separate future analysis idea for field-by-field ownership mapping.
- Cross-target wrapper consumers only when an AArch64-proven or retained-owner
  boundary already exists and the follow-up remains one surface.

## Out Of Scope

- Direct implementation.
- Direct consumer migration.
- Prepared API deletion, cache privatization, or aggregate contraction.
- Broad `PreparedFunctionLookups` retirement.
- Broad `PreparedBirModule` clone, facade, deletion, or retirement.
- Opening or executing draft 155.
- Moving target/prepared policy into route ownership without a named
  replacement owner and proof plan.
- Weakening tests, diagnostics, baselines, helper-oracle names, supported-path
  status, or expected strings.

## Required Analysis

D2 must build a retained-surface consumer dependency map from the C2 output.
For each candidate surface, record:

- C2 classification and exact retained prepared surface;
- current production, printer/debug, target-wrapper, oracle, pass-context, and
  expected-string consumers;
- whether the selected Route 3 through Route 7 adapter removed any public
  consumer boundary or only added route/prepared agreement;
- retained fallback, target/prepared policy, and diagnostic/string authority;
- what one-surface follow-up could safely improve next;
- why that follow-up is analysis-worthy or why it should be no-action;
- proof requirements including positive, absent, invalid, duplicate/conflict,
  mismatch, fallback, printer/debug, wrapper, and expected-string cases.

The analysis must keep C2 no-go decisions intact unless it records new,
surface-specific evidence from code or closure notes.

## Expected Output

The closure note must contain:

- a link to
  `docs/bir_prealloc_fusion/phase_d2_retained_surface_consumer_switch_analysis.md`;
- the retained-surface consumer dependency map;
- any accepted follow-up implementation ideas, each scoped to exactly one
  surface or diagnostic/oracle row;
- explicit no-action decisions for surfaces that remain fallback/oracle,
  target/prepared policy, pass-context, or blocked aggregate work;
- an explicit statement that draft 155 / prepared-module retirement is not
  ready unless D2 discovers and documents separate field-by-field ownership
  prerequisites;
- remaining blockers to `PreparedFunctionLookups` and `PreparedBirModule`
  retirement.

## Reviewer Reject Signals

- Treating C2 selected adapter success as cache/API contraction readiness.
- Opening a broad D2 migration ladder that ignores C2 retained-surface
  decisions.
- Producing follow-up ideas that contain more than one prepared surface or
  diagnostic/oracle row.
- Migrating consumers or deleting prepared APIs inside this analysis phase.
- Moving target policy into BIR route views without a named replacement owner
  and proof plan.
- Treating diagnostic/oracle replacement, printer/debug output, wrapper
  compatibility, baseline, or expected-string authority as optional.
- Claiming draft 155, broad `PreparedFunctionLookups`, or broad
  `PreparedBirModule` retirement is ready from C2 evidence.
- Weakening tests, diagnostics, baselines, helper-oracle names, supported-path
  status, or expected strings.
