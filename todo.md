## Just Finished

Step 7 classified the remaining `00204` segmentation fault after the ALU
repairs made `00164` pass.

The failure is in the first `stdarg()` call:
`myprintf("%9s %9s %9s %9s %9s %9s", s9, s9, s9, s9, s9, s9)`.
With unbuffered stdout the binary reaches `stdarg:` and begins the first
`%9s` line before faulting. GDB stops in `myprintf` at the generated byte load
for the `%9s` `va_arg` copy:
`myprintf+772`, assembly `ldrb w9, [x13]`, with `x13 = 0x10`.

Semantic BIR for `myprintf` lowers `%9s` as manual `va_arg` cursor logic over
`%lv.ap.24`/`%lv.ap.0` rather than an ALU source chain. The prepared caller
plan for the first `stdarg` call is coherent: `@.str49` goes to `x0`, the first
three `struct s9` byval arguments go to `x1/x2`, `x3/x4`, and `x5/x6`, and the
remaining three go to outgoing stack slots at `stack+0`, `stack+16`, and
`stack+32`.

The generated `myprintf` prologue saves `x1`..`x7` into its register-save area
and initializes `gp_offset = -56`, but the `%9s` va_arg path switches to the
overflow path with a bad cursor. In that overflow path, the assembly updates
`%lv.ap.0` from a spill slot (`ldr x9, [sp, #24]; add x9, x9, #16`) instead of
advancing from the loaded overflow pointer. The next copy dereferences `x13 =
0x10`, causing the segmentation fault.
